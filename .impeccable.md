# Design Context — Autocrew Marketing Site

## Users

- **Primary audience:** Operators at growing businesses evaluating AI that can answer on their behalf — front-desk coordinators, clinic managers, heads of support, small-business owners, ops leads.
- **Context:** Desktop-first evaluation during a workday, phones used for spot-checks between tasks. Decisions are considered, not impulsive — the product is a replacement for hiring.
- **Job to be done:** "I need to find out, in under five minutes, whether this will actually handle the volume my team can't, without a sales gauntlet." Visitors arrive skeptical of chatbot-marketing-speak.

## Brand Personality

- **Three words:** professional, approachable, warm.
- **Voice:** specific over generic. Outcome-focused sentences over feature lists. Lightly declarative — it says what it does rather than pitching. Parentheticals are used to add precision, not marketing polish.
- **Emotional goal:** the visitor should think _"these people know the work,"_ not _"this is a slick SaaS."_ Competence + human warmth, not hype.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme:** dark-primary (deep navy `#03060e` background). Light mode exists but the hero lives in dark.
- **Primary accent:** warm orange `oklch(66% 0.20 38)` ≈ `#FF6B35`. Used sparingly — it is a punctuation color, not a decorative one.
- **Type system:** Space Grotesk (display), Geist Sans (body), Space Mono (labels/status). No new display fonts introduced; variations come from weight, scale, and composition — not new families.
- **Motion:** restrained. Entrance fades + the occasional pulse. No loops, no continuous motion that competes with interactive elements.
- **Layout:** current hero is centered-single-column; the redesign is allowed to break symmetry and explore asymmetric/editorial/architectural compositions.

## Anti-References

- **Not generic SaaS-with-a-button.** The hero should not read as "big headline, two buttons, mockup below" — that's the template the visitor has seen 200 times.
- **Not playful.** No cartoon illustrations, no hand-drawn accents, no emoji, no bouncy motion.
- **Not a chatbot landing page.** No speech-bubble hero art, no anthropomorphized avatars, no "Hi, I'm Sarah 👋" as the opener.
- **No AI-slop visual tells.** No cyan-on-dark gradients, no purple-to-blue gradients, no gradient text, no border-left accent stripes, no glassmorphism-everywhere, no rounded-rect-drop-shadow cards as the dominant pattern.

## Design Principles

1. **Specificity is the vibe.** Every concrete noun (appointment, intake, escalation, patient, lead, reservation) outperforms a generic one. Copy and UI both.
2. **Orange is a verb.** The accent marks moments of action or liveness — never decoration. If a shape can be neutral, it's neutral.
3. **Hierarchy from restraint.** Space and type contrast do the work that color and ornament usually do in B2B AI marketing.
4. **Product-first, not pitch-first.** Any interactive surface (search, CTA) behaves like a working tool, not a call-to-action graphic.
5. **Room for the visitor to think.** Don't crowd the fold. Generous negative space signals confidence.
