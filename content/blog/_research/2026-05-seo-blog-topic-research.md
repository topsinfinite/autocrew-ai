## How topics were scored

Each topic carries three numeric scores (1–3) and a total (max 9). Topics ≥7/9 are recommended for a brief; 5–6 are park-for-later; <5 are discarded.

- **Commercial intent (CI)** — would a searcher buy or book a demo near the bottom of this funnel?
- **SERP opportunity (SO)** — is the current top-10 weak (thin content, dated, no direct answer, no original artifact)?
- **Autocrew fit (AF)** — does Autocrew's product directly answer the search intent better than a generic answer?

Scoring is based on the SERP and stat data captured in this research session, not on Ideabrowser MCP runs (which were unavailable in this session). Brief authors should re-validate with Ideabrowser before committing word counts.

## Tier 1 — Bottom-funnel / commercial intent (ship first)

These earn revenue per visit. The SERP for each is dominated by competitor product pages, which means Autocrew can win with a balanced, sourced, non-self-promotional treatment.

### T1-1. AI Receptionist Cost: 2026 Pricing Guide

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 3 / AF 3 = **9/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "ai receptionist cost"
- **Secondary:** "ai receptionist pricing," "how much does an ai receptionist cost," "ai answering service cost"
- **Why it ranks:** Every major competitor publishes a "cost guide" — NextPhone, Dialzara, Ringly, Aira, Callbird, AgentZap. These all ride between $25–$3,000/month with the same hand-wavy ranges. None publish a true total-cost-of-ownership breakdown including hidden fees (setup, overage, integrations) against the cost of doing nothing (missed calls).
- **Autocrew angle (own this):** Frame the comparison as **AI Receptionist vs. Hire vs. Answering Service vs. Doing Nothing**. Use Autocrew's missed-call formula (already published) to show that "doing nothing" is the most expensive option for 95% of practices. Reference: traditional answering service is $400–800/month for 200 calls, full-time hire is $4,100+/month, AI is $25–$300/month, and doing nothing is $5K–$60K/month for a typical service business.
- **Original artifact:** Comparison table with 4 cost models × 5 hidden cost lines, sourced inline.
- **Internal links:** `/ai-receptionist`, the missed-call calculator post, the AI-vs-live-answering post (T1-2)
- **Word count:** 1,800–2,200

### T1-2. AI Receptionist vs Live Answering Service (already an Idea in pipeline — promote to brief)

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 3 / AF 3 = **9/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "ai receptionist vs answering service"
- **Why it ranks:** SERP is filled with vendor blogs (Zenoti, Ionos, Vida, AgentZap, Trillet, Huskyvoice) — all argue for whichever side they sell. Autocrew can publish the only honest decision tree: "use a live service when X, use AI when Y, use Smart Escalation (Autocrew's hybrid) when Z."
- **Autocrew angle:** Smart Escalation is the unique product wedge here. Most AI-only competitors can't offer the live bridge; most live-answering competitors don't have the AI tier. Autocrew's product collapses the choice.
- **Existing pipeline status:** Marked as Idea for June 12, 2026 — should be promoted to Brief immediately.
- **Word count:** 1,500–2,000

### T1-3. Smith.ai Alternatives: 7 AI Receptionists Compared

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 3 / AF 2 = **8/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "smith.ai alternatives"
- **Secondary:** "smith.ai vs," "smith.ai competitors"
- **Why it ranks:** Smith.ai is the gravity well of branded search in this space — its name appears in every comparison post in the SERP. The current top results are written by direct competitors (Trillet, NextPhone, Quo, Eden, ServiceAgent), so they're transparently self-serving. Autocrew can publish a more balanced comparison and still feature itself fairly (Smart Escalation is a defensible point of difference vs. Smith's hybrid model).
- **Autocrew angle:** Position Smith.ai as the hybrid leader, name 5–7 honest alternatives (Rosie, Ruby, AnswerConnect, Trillet, Dialzara, Autocrew) with transparent strength/weakness across pricing, training time, integrations. Lead the table with a "best for healthcare with EHR" recommendation that lands on Autocrew without being aggressive.
- **Risk:** Trademark sensitivity. Use "alternatives" not "vs," and stick to publicly published facts only.
- **Companion piece:** A second comparison post for "best AI receptionist for [vertical]" once T1-3 ranks.
- **Word count:** 2,000–2,500 (this is a list/comparison pillar — long form is the convention)

### T1-4. Best AI Receptionist for Small Business (2026 Comparison)

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 2 / AF 2 = **7/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "best ai receptionist"
- **Secondary:** "best ai virtual receptionist," "top ai receptionists"
- **Why it ranks:** Same SERP dynamics as T1-3 but broader keyword. NextPhone, Cekura, Bookipi, Retell, Cloudtalk, AgentZap, ALM all publish "best AI receptionist" lists. Most are list-of-10 affiliate-style posts. A genuinely curated "by use case" cut (best for healthcare, best for coaching studios, best for restaurants, best for hybrid AI+human) can break in.
- **Autocrew angle:** Use the four wedge verticals as the comparison frame, not "top 10." Each vertical recommendation lands on a different competitor strength so the post doesn't read as a self-pitch. Autocrew is "best for HIPAA-aware healthcare with EHR integration."
- **Word count:** 2,000–2,500

### T1-5. AI Receptionist Pricing Calculator (interactive page, not a post)

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 3 / AF 3 = **9/9** (as a long-tail trap)
- **Primary keyword:** "ai receptionist cost calculator"
- **Format:** Interactive calculator page at `/tools/ai-receptionist-cost-calculator` with 4 inputs (calls/day, avg deal value, conversion %, current solution) and an output showing monthly cost vs. monthly missed-call leak.
- **Why it ranks:** No competitor in this SERP publishes an interactive calculator that compares cost-of-AI to cost-of-doing-nothing. The closest is Callbird's static "ROI calculator" inside a blog post.
- **Autocrew angle:** This is the linkable asset that earns backlinks for the next two years. Pair it with T1-1 (the cost guide post) — guide ranks for "cost," calculator earns the links and direct conversions.
- **Build cost:** ~3 days of dev (already exists as a missed-call calculator concept; extend it).

## Tier 2 — Compliance, risk, and "is this safe" content

Buyers in healthcare, legal, and finance can't sign without compliance answers. Compliance content also produces the highest-intent leads in the funnel — anyone searching "HIPAA voice AI" is past the "is this real" stage.

### T2-1. HIPAA Compliance for Voice AI: A 2026 Buyer Checklist

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 3 / AF 3 = **9/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "hipaa compliant voice ai"
- **Secondary:** "hipaa ai phone agent," "BAA voice ai," "ai receptionist hipaa"
- **Why it ranks:** Top-10 is split between vendor pages (Vapi, Retell, Linear, Simbie, Hamming, Bluejay, Dialzara) and one PMC academic article. None publish a buyer-ready compliance checklist with the actual five technical controls (TLS 1.2+, AES-256, RBAC, 6-yr audit logs, breach notification window) framed as "ask your vendor these questions before signing."
- **Autocrew angle:** Autocrew already has HIPAA-aware healthcare deployments and the EHR integration story (Epic, Cerner, Athenahealth named on the home page). A buyer checklist that includes "does the vendor sign a BAA" and "what happens to PHI when the contract ends" is a sales-enablement asset disguised as a blog post.
- **Internal links:** `/industry/healthcare`, the after-hours healthcare post
- **Word count:** 1,800–2,200

### T2-2. TCPA Compliance for AI Voice Agents in 2026

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 3 / AF 2 = **8/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "tcpa ai voice"
- **Secondary:** "ai robocall consent," "ai voice agent fcc rules," "outbound ai voice tcpa"
- **Why it ranks:** The FCC ruled in 2024 that TCPA applies to AI-generated voices, with $500–$1,500 statutory damages per call. The NPRM may finalize in 2026. The SERP is currently dominated by law-firm pages and one or two vendor blogs (Kixie, AgentVoice, Henson Legal). A practitioner checklist for service businesses ("when do you need consent for outbound AI calls?") fills a real gap.
- **Autocrew angle:** Autocrew leans inbound — that's the safe side of this rule. Frame the post as "what's safe to do with AI voice in 2026 (inbound + EBR exemptions)" and "what to avoid (cold-outreach AI calls without express written consent)."
- **Risk:** Legal accuracy matters. Run draft past a legal reviewer or cite Henson Legal / FCC primary sources only.
- **Word count:** 1,500–1,800

### T2-3. PCI Compliance & Voice AI: Taking Payments Over the Phone

- **Score:** CI 2 / SO 3 / AF 2 = **7/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "ai voice agent take payment pci"
- **Why it ranks:** Almost nobody covers this. Every coaching, medspa, and restaurant prospect asks "can the AI take a deposit over the phone." A short, clear "yes/no/here's how" piece has no real SERP competition and converts on commercial intent.
- **Autocrew angle:** Defer payment to a vetted PCI processor (Stripe, Square) and escalate to a human only when the caller insists on giving a card number to the agent. The escalation is Smart Escalation — exactly Autocrew's wedge.
- **Word count:** 1,000–1,400 (playbook length)

## Tier 3 — Vertical expansion (new verticals where Autocrew fits)

The current pipeline owns the four wedge verticals. The same underlying product fits at least three more without product changes — these earn easy first-page rankings because the SERP is younger and thinner than the wedge verticals.

### T3-1. AI Receptionist for Home Services (HVAC / Plumbing / Electrical)

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 3 / AF 3 = **9/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "ai receptionist home services" / "ai answering service plumber"
- **Why it ranks:** Home services is the largest underserved vertical for AI receptionists. Source data: HVAC/plumbing/electrical companies miss ~27% of incoming calls; on a $450 service ticket, missing two emergencies a week is $46K/year lost. Existing competitor coverage is heavy (CallBird, Avoca, Retell, LeadTruffle, Whippy) but few publish a vertical pillar with sourced numbers.
- **Autocrew angle:** Emergency-call recognition + dispatch routing. Smart Escalation routes a leaking-pipe call straight to the on-call tech; routine "quote me a tune-up" calls book themselves.
- **Required:** A new `/industry/home-services` page in the product surface (this post's internal-link target). Worth shipping the page first.
- **Word count:** 2,000–2,500

### T3-2. AI Receptionist for Veterinary Clinics

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 3 / AF 3 = **9/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "ai receptionist veterinary clinic"
- **Why it ranks:** Vet clinics miss 20–30% of calls during busy hours; "telephone access is the number one source of complaints" in UK/Irish vet practices. Vertical SERP is thin — a few vendors (VoiceFleet, Dialzara, DaVoice, Dodo) and almost no editorial.
- **Autocrew angle:** Vet calls are emotionally charged (sick pet, end-of-life, emergency). Autocrew's tone controls + Smart Escalation handle the emotion-vs-routine sort. Good demo flow for sales.
- **Required:** `/industry/veterinary` page (or fold into a broader "specialty practices" page).
- **Word count:** 2,000–2,500

### T3-3. AI Receptionist for Medspas & Aesthetics Clinics

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 2 / AF 3 = **8/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "ai receptionist medspa"
- **Why it ranks:** Higher CAC than dental, longer consult-to-book cycle, callers shop multiple clinics. Existing competitors (Zenoti, Spa Voices, MySalonDesk, AnswerBug) are mostly point solutions. Autocrew can frame as "consult-to-treatment booking flow" rather than "answer the phone."
- **Autocrew angle:** Two-step booking — qualify on first call, book consultation, send pre-treatment paperwork via SMS/email automation. Multi-step product surface for a high-AOV vertical.
- **Word count:** 1,500–2,000

### T3-4. AI Receptionist for Real Estate / Property Management

- **Score:** CI 2 / SO 3 / AF 2 = **7/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "ai receptionist real estate" / "ai receptionist property management"
- **Why it ranks:** Underserved vertical with high call volume (showing requests, maintenance tickets, leasing inquiries). SERP has almost no dedicated content; a vertical pillar can rank with average effort.
- **Autocrew angle:** Maintenance ticket triage (renter calls "AC is broken" → Smart Escalation routes to the property's maintenance vendor). Showing requests book directly to agent calendars.
- **Word count:** 1,500–2,000

These are the posts a sales rep sends after a demo. They convert at higher rates than top-of-funnel content because the reader has already accepted the premise.

- **Why it ranks:** SERP is dominated by vendor pages with generic advice ("use clear language"). A real, named-vertical prompt template — coaching studio greeting, dental front-desk greeting, legal intake greeting — is rare.
- **Autocrew angle:** Three full prompt examples drawn from the four wedge verticals plus a one-page "prompt teardown" of a real Autocrew deployment. This is also a signup magnet — readers want the templates.
- **Word count:** 1,500–2,000

### T4-2. AI Receptionist Setup: A 30-Minute Implementation Guide

- **Score:** CI 2 / SO 2 / AF 3 = **7/9**

- **Why it ranks:** SERP has competitor setup guides (Loman, Voiceflow, MyAIFrontDesk, NextPhone) and one Microsoft Azure tutorial. None walk through the Autocrew-style "live in days" four-step flow framed as a workshop.

- **Word count:** 1,200–1,600 (playbook)

### T4-3. EHR Integration for Voice AI: Epic, Cerner, Athenahealth (Healthcare-Specific)

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 2 / AF 3 = **8/9**

### T4-4. Smart Escalation: When AI Should Hand the Call to a Human

- **Score:** CI 2 / SO 3 / AF 3 = **8/9**
- **Primary keyword:** "ai voice agent escalation" / "ai live transfer"
- **Why it ranks:** Smart Escalation is Autocrew's most distinctive feature on the home page, but no SERP currently owns the question "when should an AI agent escalate?" Most competitor content treats escalation as a footnote. A pillar post that defines the four trigger types (caller frustration, intent ambiguity, compliance, requested human) creates a defensible point of view.
- **Autocrew angle:** Direct product moat. This post functions as a quasi-product page.
- **Word count:** 1,500–2,000

## Tier 5 — Linkable assets and original research (slow build, durable rankings)

These do not rank fast. They earn backlinks for years, which lift everything else on the domain. Schedule one per quarter.

### T5-1. The Autocrew 2026 Missed Call Benchmark Report

- **Score:** CI 2 / SO 3 / AF 3 = **8/9**
- **Format:** Original-research PDF + landing page
- **Method:** Aggregate anonymized call-volume data from Autocrew deployments (with permission), publish a benchmark by vertical: % of calls missed, average response time, after-hours share, ticket size of missed call. Even a sample of 20–30 deployments gives a publishable benchmark.
- **Why it builds links:** SERP for "missed call statistics" is dominated by recycled 2019 figures (the famous "$75 per missed call" number is from a 2010 Forbes piece). Fresh, sourced numbers from a 2026 dataset are immediately citable by every competitor blog and law-firm marketing site.
- **Comparable pieces:** Peerlogic published a 4,280-call dental dataset in February 2026 and got cited in a dozen industry blogs within 60 days.
- **Cost:** 2–3 weeks of work — but produces the highest-ROI single asset on this list.

### T5-2. Missed Call Cost Calculator (interactive widget, embeddable)

- **Score:** CI 3 / SO 3 / AF 3 = **9/9**
- **Format:** Interactive page, plus an embeddable iframe other sites can paste in
- **Why it builds links:** Same SERP gap as T1-5 above. The embed multiplies the link value — anyone embedding the widget gets a do-follow link back to Autocrew.
- **Pair with:** Existing missed-call calculator post (May 1) and the new pricing guide (T1-1).

### T5-3. Voice AI Adoption Survey: 500 Service Businesses, 2026

- **Score:** CI 1 / SO 3 / AF 2 = **6/9** (longer-term)
- **Format:** Survey + report
- **Why it ranks:** Most adoption stats in the SERP cite G2's annual report, Gartner forecasts, or Nextiva's stats roundup. A first-party Autocrew survey of small service businesses on AI receptionist adoption (intent, blockers, what they actually use) gets cited and produces the press hook for a Q4 launch.
- **Cost:** ~$2K for a 500-respondent panel via Pollfish or Prolific.

## What this is missing (caveats)

1. **No Ideabrowser MCP data.** This research used public web search and SERP inspection. Topics should be re-validated in Ideabrowser before briefs are written, per `RESEARCH-WORKFLOW.md`. The scores here are a planning aid, not a substitute.
2. **No first-party search-console data.** If Autocrew has ≥30 days of GSC data on the existing five posts, the next planning round should pull "queries we already rank for between #11 and #30" — those are the cheapest wins.
3. **No competitor traffic data.** Ideal next step: pull Ahrefs / Semrush snapshots on the 6–8 direct competitor blogs (Smith.ai, NextPhone, Dialzara, Trillet, Loman, Voiceflow, Avoca, Retell). Flag any keywords where they rank top-5 with thin content.
4. **Internal-link budget.** Several of these posts assume product surface that may not exist yet (`/industry/home-services`, `/industry/veterinary`). Coordinate the page builds before scheduling the posts; otherwise the posts ship with broken canonical link targets.
5. **Compliance review.** T2-1 (HIPAA) and T2-2 (TCPA) need a legal reviewer before publish — these are the posts most likely to be screenshotted by buyers and used in vendor due-diligence.
