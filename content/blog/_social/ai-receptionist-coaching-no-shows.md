### Variant A — Narrative hook

A no-show isn't a missed session.

It's a 60-minute hole in your schedule that you almost certainly couldn't fill on short notice — plus the 15 minutes of follow-up email you'll send to reschedule.

Here's how no-shows actually happen:

Your client opens your reminder email at 7am. They realize they have a conflict. They mean to reply. They get pulled into a meeting. They forget. The session time arrives. They're not there.

It's not that they don't value the work. It's that the rescheduling loop has too much friction.

Voice reminder calls close the loop in under 90 seconds:

"Hi [Name], confirming your session tomorrow at 2pm. Press 1 to confirm, 2 to reschedule."

The client presses 2. The agent immediately offers three open slots from your calendar. The client picks one. Booked. Confirmation text sent. Your calendar updates in real time.

Voice confirmation calls hit 70-85%. SMS reminders hit 40-55%. The gap closes about a third of your no-shows on its own.

The same agent handles the other half of the scheduling problem — prospective clients calling at 9pm after a breakthrough conversation over dinner. That call doesn't need to go to voicemail. The agent describes your programs, answers pricing questions, and books the discovery call directly.

You wake up to a confirmed booking, not a missed lead.

### Variant B — Data/insight

Most coaches accept 15-20% no-shows as a cost of doing business.

It's not.

On a full coaching calendar of 20 sessions per week at $150 per session, 15-20% no-shows is $450-$600 of weekly revenue evaporating before the first call starts. That's $23,000-$31,000 a year disappearing because rescheduling has too much friction.

Three numbers that change the picture:

- Voice reminder calls confirm at 70-85%. SMS reminders confirm at 40-55%.
- A typical coach with 3-4 weekly cancellations spends 18-60 minutes on rescheduling logistics each week. Over a year, 15-52 hours.
- The reschedule completes in under 90 seconds when the agent has real-time calendar access. The client picks a slot, the agent books it, the confirmation text sends. No email thread.

The instinct most coaches have is that automating reminders feels impersonal. Sending a reminder text isn't a relationship-building moment. Freeing yourself from that admin layer creates more space for the moments that are.

The setup has three pieces: knowledge base (your programs, pricing, ideal client), schedule integration (Calendly, Acuity, Practice, CoachVantage — all standard), and three call flows (reminder, reschedule, new client intake).

Most coaches finish in under two hours.
