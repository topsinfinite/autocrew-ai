### Variant A — Narrative hook

Friday at 7:30 PM.

Your dining room is full. Your host is managing a 20-minute waitlist and seating three tables simultaneously.

The phone rings. It rings again. And again.

The caller — trying to book for Saturday night — hangs up and opens OpenTable. If you're lucky, they find your restaurant there. If you're not, they book somewhere else.

This is the restaurant reservation overflow problem. It doesn't happen on slow Tuesdays. It happens on the nights that matter most.

The math: a typical full-service restaurant gets 20-35 reservation calls on a Friday or Saturday evening. During peak service hours, your host's answer rate is 30-50% at best. That's 10-17 unanswered calls on your busiest nights.

Even at 50% conversion and a $60 average per cover for a party of three, that's $900-$1,500 walking out the door before you open. Every Saturday.

The instinct is to hire another host or demand the existing team answer the phone while seating guests. Neither works. Adding a host for phones is wasteful on slow nights. Asking your team to answer calls while running the floor produces distracted service and partially-answered calls.

The phone has always been the weakest link in restaurant hospitality. Your team is built for in-person service.

An AI agent on your reservation line — connected to OpenTable, Resy, SevenRooms, Yelp, or Tock — answers in your restaurant's voice, checks live availability, books the reservation, and captures special requests (birthday, allergy, seating preference) before the guests arrive.

For most restaurants, the easy first step is after-hours coverage alone — typically 18+ hours/day of uncovered call time. That window adds 15-30 confirmed reservations per week for a mid-volume restaurant.

### Variant B — Data/insight

That's the structural problem at the heart of restaurant reservation overflow.

The numbers:

- A typical full-service restaurant: 20-35 reservation calls on Friday/Saturday evening
- Host answer rate during peak service (6-9pm): 30-50%
- Unanswered calls per busy night: 10-17
- Revenue walking away nightly (3-top, $60/cover, 50% conversion): $900-$1,500

Three things break when you try to fix this with people:

1. Hiring a phone host is wasteful on slow nights and you can't always staff up for Friday alone.
2. Asking the existing team to answer while seating produces distracted service and half-finished calls.
3. Phone reservation management is a different skill set from in-person hospitality. Your hosts were hired for the room.

An AI reservation agent does one thing with exceptional consistency: it converts calls into confirmed bookings. It checks live availability in OpenTable, Resy, SevenRooms, Yelp, or Tock. It offers alternatives if the requested time is full. It captures the birthday, the nut allergy, the seating preference. The guest hangs up with a confirmation text.

After-hours alone (18+ hours/day uncovered) typically adds 15-30 reservations per week for a mid-volume restaurant.
