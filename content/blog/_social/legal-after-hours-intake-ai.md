### Variant A — Narrative hook

A personal injury client calls your firm at 9:47 PM on a Thursday, two weeks after a car accident.

They've been putting it off, but tonight they finally picked up the phone.

They reach voicemail.

According to research by Clio, 70% of prospective legal clients who hit voicemail don't leave a message. Of those who do, fewer than half receive a response within 24 hours.

The client you just missed? On a contingency case, they might have been worth $40,000 in fees. They're calling another firm tomorrow.

Law firms talk about intake as an administrative function. It is, in practice, a revenue function. Every prospective client who calls and doesn't connect with a human — or a capable virtual alternative — is a potential case you'll never see.

Answering services don't fix this. They take messages. They don't qualify leads, ask about the incident date, check for statute of limitations risk, or book consultations.

A properly configured legal AI intake agent does:

It identifies as a virtual assistant (not an attorney). It runs a practice-area-specific intake interview — the questions you'd ask for personal injury are different from family law or criminal defense. It qualifies the lead against your criteria. It books the consultation directly into your calendar. It logs everything to Clio, MyCase, Practice Panther, or Filevine.

For statute-of-limitations cases, it flags the call for immediate attorney notification rather than waiting for morning review.

The competitive piece nobody talks about: in most practice areas, the first attorney to substantively engage with a prospective client has a significant advantage. Legal consumers often retain whoever responds first.

### Variant B — Data/insight

70% of prospective legal clients who reach voicemail don't leave a message. (Clio)

On a contingency case worth $25,000-$75,000 in fees, that one missed call is the most expensive minute of your week.

Three operational realities most firms underweight:

1. Personal injury, family law, and criminal defense clients call at the moment they decide to act — often 7pm to midnight, often immediately after an event or during a crisis. The after-hours window is where a meaningful share of potential clients make first contact.

2. Answering services don't qualify leads. They take messages. By the time your intake team returns the call at 11am, the client has spoken to two competitors and retained the one who picked up.

3. The first attorney to engage substantively often wins the case. Legal consumers don't comparison-shop the way they shop for products. They retain whoever responds.

A properly configured AI intake agent runs a practice-area-specific intake (different questions for personal injury vs. family law vs. criminal defense), qualifies against your criteria, books consultations directly, and flags statute-of-limitations risk for immediate attorney notification.

It is explicitly not allowed to give legal advice. The agent collects information, answers logistics questions (location, hours, fees), and schedules the consultation. Legal judgment stays with the attorney.

Integrates with Clio, MyCase, Practice Panther, Filevine. Setup is typically 1-2 weeks.
